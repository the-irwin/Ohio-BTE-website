Frequently Asked Questions

What is Build The Earth?

"Our mission is to fully recreate the entire Earth in Minecraft at a 1:1 scale. One block in Minecraft equates to roughly one meter in the real world, meaning that this project will fully recreate the size of our planet. Anyone is able to join us and contribute to the largest and most expansive build project to ever have been attempted in Minecraft. Every language, nationality, and regional difference is accepted and regarded as our greatest attribute as we continue our journey to unite all of Humanity's greatest achievements into a single Minecraft world." (from BuildTheEarth.net)

How can I help out?

Start by joining the Global Build The Earth and the Ohio Build The Earth discord servers.

What if I can't commit that much time to the project?

That's fine! We still encourage you to join our discord server. We welcome any amount of help, no matter how small.

What do I need to download to start building?

Join the Global Build the Earth discord server, and you can download an automatic installer from the #downloads channel. All mods, configuration, and settings are preconfigured for convenience. Note that you must have an official copy of minecraft, since unofficial/cracked versions of Minecraft are not allowed in the project.

Do I need to commit to building to join the discord servers?

Although we encourage anyone who is interested to go ahead and apply to be on our build team, if you are only interested in observing, you are welcome to join our discord servers.

How do I access the minecraft server?

Join the Ohio Build the Earth discord server, and instructions for applying to the build team can be found in the #join-the-team channel. Once you are accepted, you will be able to access the minecraft server.

What do I need to download to start building?

Join the Global Build the Earth discord server, and you can download an automatic installer from the #downloads channel. All mods, configuration, and settings are preconfigured for convenience. Note that you must have an official copy of minecraft, since unofficial/cracked versions of Minecraft are not allowed in the project.

How long will this take?

We expect this to take a few years at least, since Ohio is pretty big. However, we don't require builders to stick around from start to finish. We are flexible, and if you can only help for the next few months, we encourage you to come and help!
